📦 Concept Compressed

The Simple Present Tense describes regular actions, habits, general truths, and permanent situations. It uses the base form of the verb, adding -s, -es, or -ies for third-person singular subjects (he, she, it).

Formation

  • Structure (Affirmative): Subject + Verb(s/es) + Object
  • Structure (Negative): Subject + do/does not + Verb (base) + Object
  • Structure (Question): Do/Does + Subject + Verb (base) + Object?

When to Use

  • Habits and routines: I drink coffee every morning.
  • General facts: The Earth orbits the Sun.
  • Permanent situations: She works at a bank.
  • Scheduled events: The train leaves at 9 AM.

Time Markers

Always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/year, on Mondays, in the morning
