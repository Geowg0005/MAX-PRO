📦 Concept Compressed

The Simple Future Tense expresses actions or states that will happen in the future. English has two main forms: will and be going to, each with distinct uses.

Formation

  • Will: Subject + will + Verb (base)
  • Be going to: Subject + am/is/are going to + Verb (base)

When to Use

Form Use Example
will Spontaneous decisions, promises, predictions I will help you. / It will rain tomorrow.
be going to Planned actions, intentions, evidence-based predictions I am going to study tonight. / Look at those clouds—it is going to rain.

Common Time Markers

Tomorrow, next week/month/year, soon, later, in the future, in [future time]
