📦 Concept Compressed

Must, have to, and need to all express obligation or necessity, but differ in formality, source of obligation, and meaning in negative forms.

Positive Forms: Obligation/Necessity

Modal Meaning Example
must Personal feeling / Rules I must finish this today.
have to External rule (School/Job) Students have to wear uniforms.
need to Neutral necessity I need to buy groceries.

Negative Forms: VERY DIFFERENT!

Negative Form Meaning Example
must not PROHIBITION (Forbidden) You must not smoke here.
do not have to OPTIONAL (Not necessary) You do not have to come.

Critical Rule

  • Must not: You are forbidden to do it.
  • Do not have to: You are not required to do it (it's your choice).
