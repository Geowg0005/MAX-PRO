📖 📚 HKDSE Reading - Tips & Strategies

You have 1 hour 15 minutes to read 3 long passages. You CANNOT read every word. You must read FAST and SMART using proper techniques and time management.

⚡ Part 1: Skimming & Scanning Techniques

What is the Difference?

Technique What It Is When to Use Time Needed
Skimming Read very FAST to get the MAIN IDEA First time reading the passage 1-2 minutes per passage
Scanning Look for ONE specific piece of information When answering a specific question 30 seconds to 1 minute per question

Technique 1: SKIMMING (Getting the Main Idea)

Skimming means reading FAST without reading every word. You get the BIG PICTURE.

🎯 How to Skim a Passage (Follow These Steps):

  1. Read the title and heading first. This tells you what the passage is about.
  2. Read the first paragraph completely. This introduces the main idea.
  3. Read the first sentence of each middle paragraph. Topic sentences tell you what each paragraph is about.
  4. Read the last paragraph completely. This summarizes the main idea.
  5. Look at any images, charts, or diagrams. They give you important information.
  6. Do NOT read every sentence. You are just finding the main idea.

✓ Example: How to Skim

WRONG (Reading every word - takes 5 minutes):
"The ancient civilization of Egypt was located in the northeastern part of Africa. The Nile River was very important to the Egyptians because it provided water and fertile soil for farming. The Egyptians built many pyramids as tombs for their pharaohs. The pyramids were very large structures that took many workers and many years to build..."

RIGHT (Skimming - takes 30 seconds):
Read: "The ancient civilization of Egypt..." [First paragraph]
Read: "...The Nile River was very important..." [Key detail]
Read: "The pyramids were..." [Topic sentence only]
Understanding: This is about Ancient Egypt and its pyramids.

Technique 2: SCANNING (Finding Specific Information)

Scanning means looking for ONE SPECIFIC ANSWER without reading everything.

🎯 How to Scan for an Answer:

  1. Read the question carefully. Know exactly what information you need.
  2. Find keywords in the question. These words will help you find the answer.
  3. Look for those keywords in the passage. Your eyes move FAST across the text.
  4. When you find a keyword, read the sentence around it carefully. The answer is usually there.
  5. Move on to the next question. Don't waste time re-reading.

Practice Scanning

Question: "When was the Eiffel Tower built?"

Passage: "The Eiffel Tower is located in Paris, France. It was designed by Gustave Eiffel in 1889. The tower stands 330 meters tall. Many tourists visit it every year..."

How to Scan:

  • Question asks: WHEN (looking for a year/date)
  • Keywords: "Eiffel Tower" + year
  • Scan for: "Eiffel Tower" → Found it in passage
  • Read sentence: "...in 1889."
  • Answer: 1889 ✓

Question Types and Scanning Strategies

WHO Questions (People)

Question: "Who did X?"

  • Scan for: Action verb (did, wrote, created, built)
  • The person's name comes before or after the verb
  • Example: "Einstein developed the theory of relativity" - WHO = Einstein

WHAT Questions (Things/Ideas)

Question: "What is X?" or "What happened?"

  • Scan for: The word or concept in the question
  • Read the definition or description in the passage
  • Example: "What is photosynthesis?" - Find the word "photosynthesis" and read the definition

WHEN Questions (Time)

Question: "When did X happen?"

  • Scan for: Numbers, years, dates, months, days
  • Look for time words: "in", "on", "during", "before", "after"
  • Example: "When was the Internet invented?" - Scan for years near "Internet"

WHERE Questions (Places)

Question: "Where is X?"

  • Scan for: Place names, locations, countries, cities
  • Look for: Capitalized words (proper nouns)
  • Example: "Where is the Statue of Liberty?" - Scan for "Statue of Liberty" and read the location

WHY Questions (Reasons)

Question: "Why did X happen?"

  • Scan for: "because", "due to", "caused by", "reason"
  • These words introduce the reason
  • Example: "Why did she leave?" - Find "because" and read what follows

Common Scanning Mistakes

❌ Don't Make These Mistakes:

  • Don't read the whole passage for each question. This wastes time.
  • Don't stop at the first keyword you find. Make sure the sentence answers the question.
  • Don't assume you know the answer. Always check the passage first.
  • Don't scan too slowly. Move your eyes fast across the page.
  • Don't skip the skimming step. Understanding the main idea helps you scan faster.

Summary: When to Use Each Technique

Situation Use This Technique What You Do
First time reading a passage SKIM Read title, first paragraph, topic sentences, last paragraph (2 min)
Answering a specific question SCAN Look for keywords, find the sentence, read the answer (1 min)
Confused about main idea SKIM again Re-read first and last paragraphs to refresh your memory
You have 5 seconds left SCAN only Look for one keyword and guess the best answer

⏱️ Part 2: Time Management Strategies

The HKDSE Reading Paper has 3 long passages and many questions. You have only 1 hour 15 minutes (75 minutes total). You MUST manage your time carefully or you will NOT finish.

The Big Picture: 75 Minutes for 3 Passages

⏰ Total Time Breakdown:

Activity Time Notes
Reading and answering all 3 passages 65 minutes Skim, read, answer questions
Checking answers 10 minutes Fix mistakes, review difficult questions
Total 75 minutes Don't go over!

Passage Types and Time Allocation

Different passages are different difficulty levels and have different numbers of questions.

🎯 Typical HKDSE Reading Paper Structure:

Passage Type Difficulty Questions Time
Passage 1 Short article / Advertisement / Email EASY 5-7 questions 8-10 min
Passage 2 Medium article / Blog / News report MEDIUM 8-10 questions 20-25 min
Passage 3 Long article / Academic text / Complex text HARD 12-15 questions 25-30 min

Detailed Time Strategy for Each Passage

Passage 1: 8-10 Minutes (Easy)

Step Activity Time
1 Skim the passage (title, first sentence, format) 1 minute
2 Read all questions first (predict answers) 2 minutes
3 Scan for answers and complete questions 5 minutes
4 Review your answers (check for mistakes) 1 minute

Total: 8-10 minutes ✓ You now have 65 minutes left for passages 2 and 3.

Passage 2: 20-25 Minutes (Medium)

Step Activity Time
1 Skim passage (title, first + last paragraph, topic sentences) 2 minutes
2 Read all questions (understand what they ask) 2 minutes
3 Read the passage more carefully this time 8 minutes
4 Answer all questions (scan + write answers) 10 minutes
5 Quick check of difficult answers 2 minutes

Total: 20-25 minutes ✓ You now have 40 minutes for passage 3 (perfect!).

Passage 3: 25-30 Minutes (Hard)

Step Activity Time
1 Skim passage (get main idea) 2 minutes
2 Read all questions carefully (this passage is hard!) 3 minutes
3 Read the passage completely (word by word for this one) 10 minutes
4 Answer questions (scan for specific information) 12 minutes
5 Final check on answers you weren't sure about 3 minutes

Total: 25-30 minutes ✓ Done with 5-10 minutes for final review!

How to Answer Questions Quickly

Multiple Choice Questions (Choose A, B, C, or D)

Time: 30-45 seconds per question
  1. Read the question. Know what you're looking for.
  2. Find the keywords in the passage. Use scanning (1-2 seconds)
  3. Read the sentence with the keyword. (5-10 seconds)
  4. Check all 4 options quickly. (10-15 seconds)
  5. Choose the best answer. (5 seconds)
  6. Move on. Don't second-guess yourself.

Short Answer Questions (Write 1-3 words)

Time: 1-2 minutes per question
  1. Find the answer in the passage. Usually very close to keywords.
  2. Copy words from the passage. Don't worry about perfect grammar.
  3. Use as few words as possible. "1-3 words" means SHORT answers.
  4. Check spelling quickly. But don't worry about perfect spelling.
  5. Move on. Accuracy is more important than perfection.

Gap-Filling / Matching Questions

Time: 1-2 minutes per question
  1. Read what goes in the gap. Should it be a noun? Verb? Adjective?
  2. Scan for the missing word. Look nearby (usually in next 2-3 sentences)
  3. Check if the word fits grammatically. Does it make sense?
  4. Fill in the gap and move on. Don't overthink.

Common Time Management Mistakes

❌ Don't Make These Mistakes:

  • Don't spend too much time on passage 1. It's easy - move quickly!
  • Don't read every word. Use skimming and scanning to save time.
  • Don't get stuck on 1 difficult question. Skip it and come back at the end.
  • Don't re-read the entire passage for each question. Scan quickly instead.
  • Don't spend 20+ minutes on passage 3. Leave yourself time to check answers.
  • Don't run out of time. If you have 5 minutes left, you can't answer 10 questions!

What If You're Running Out of Time?

⚠️ Emergency Time Management:

  • 5 minutes left for passage 3? Skip the difficult questions. Use scanning for quick answers in easier questions.
  • Haven't started passage 3? Skim VERY FAST (1 minute). Answer only questions about facts/numbers/names (easier to scan).
  • Multiple choice questions left? Use your best guess and pattern recognition. Don't leave blanks.
  • Short answer left? Write ANY answer. Even a wrong answer gives you a chance to get points.
  • 1 minute left? Check: Do all my blanks have answers? No? Fill in with anything. NEVER leave a blank.

Practice: Time Your Reading

✓ How to Practice Time Management:

  1. Get a timer or use your phone.
  2. Set 75 minutes. This is your exam time.
  3. Complete a full practice exam. All 3 passages, all questions.
  4. Follow the time strategy above. Stick to the time limits exactly.
  5. Write down your time for each passage. Was it more or less than planned?
  6. Review: Which passages are too slow? Passage 1 too slow? You need to skim faster. Passage 3? You need better scanning skills.
  7. Do this 5-10 times. Eventually you'll develop good time sense.

Remember: Quantity Over Quality

For the HKDSE reading exam:

  • Getting 70% of questions answered = about 70 points
  • Getting 50% of questions answered = about 50 points
  • Getting 0% of questions answered = 0 points (because you didn't finish)

Always finish the exam. Even if your answers aren't perfect, at least you answered all questions and got some points. If you don't finish, you lose all points from the questions you didn't answer!
